# My experience with writing

When it comes to my experience with 'technical' writing, I had a strong start (if you could call some of my early 'works' technical) where I blasted out a series of blogs mainly centered around my knewly acquired Raspberry Pi's and my quest to gain knowledge of Kubernetes. Please don't check them out. Sadly, since then i've neglected any technical writing for my own pleasure or the pursuit of knowledge. Since those first few articles i've written many more lines of code, worked at a variety of different places and learnt a whole lot more. After some reflection on the past year of work, I made it one of my goals to get my blog going again and start writing. Naturally, I procrastinated by creating my own blog from scratch to avoid the actual *writing* part but I did manage to learn a thing or two along the way.

# Motivations

So, if its not already clear, one of the main motivations behind building my own site is that it gives me somewhere to dump technical / non-technical ramblings, personal projects and maybe even more. I can easily tailor what I dump here and how its presented as I know and understand the code. Whilst there is a whole host of ready OOTB solutions to use here, its just not as fun really is it. My early blogs used Hugo, Ghost, Astro and I deployed them on Netfliy - a stack I highly recommend, as they all provide the tools to get going quickly and the internet is awash with tutorials. I'd be lying if I didn't say some of their tooling didn't inspire me when building out this one. The aim for this site, at least to begin with is, as little frills as possible. I wanted to use a simple stack that is self-serving and cheap to run.

# Stack

I've opted for the stack of Golang for both backend and frontend, Cloudflare for DNS, Github actions for CICD and GCP for deployments. In picking this stack, i've been fairly price conscious as they all offer a generous free tier - up to now the only thing i've paid for is my domain. But also, they're industry standard - we use all these technologies at my place of work in some form or another and they work well. It gives me the chance to dig deeper into the stack in ways I might not normally do in my day-to-day work and the opportunity to experiment.

# To sum it up

- Wanted to somewhere to dump technical articles
- Learn more about the stack
- Get better at writing
- Experiment
- Challenge myself
- Inspiration
- Guides for people - i know i use blogs as guides
- In creating this, motivate myself to create projects and be proud of the work i'm doing
- And most of all... Have fun!
